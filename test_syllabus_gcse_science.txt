GCSE Combined Science (Trilogy) Syllabus

Biology Component:
Unit B1: Cell Biology
- Cell structure and organization
- Cell division (mitosis and meiosis)  
- Organisation and the digestive system
- Organelles and their functions
- Transport in cells

Unit B2: Organisation
- Human organ systems
- Plant tissues and organs
- Disease and the immune system
- Photosynthesis and respiration
- Homeostasis and response

Chemistry Component:
Unit C1: Atomic Structure and Periodic Table
- Atomic structure and isotopes
- Electronic structure and bonding
- Groups in the periodic table
- Chemical equations and calculations
- States of matter and particle theory

Unit C2: Bonding and Reactions
- Ionic, covalent and metallic bonding
- Chemical reactions and energy changes
- Rate of reaction and catalysts
- Reversible reactions and equilibrium
- Acids, bases and pH

Physics Component:
Unit P1: Energy and Matter
- Energy stores and transfers
- Conservation of energy
- Power and efficiency
- Thermal physics and particle model
- Atomic structure and radioactivity

Unit P2: Forces and Motion
- Scalar and vector quantities
- Newton's laws of motion
- Momentum and collision
- Work, energy and power
- Waves and electromagnetic spectrum

Assessment for Ages 14-16:
Foundation Tier: Grades 1-5
Higher Tier: Grades 4-9
Six examination papers (2 per science)
